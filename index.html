What is Banana Bread?

Banana bread, made from mashed bananas, is a sweet and moist bread.

Banana bread does not need time to rise, so it is known as a quick bread. The baking soda/powder is what makes it rise so quickly.

The History of Banana Bread

In the 1800s, bananas were only available very sparingly because they are not native to North America. Bananas ripe and rotten very quickly, so it was difficult to get them across seas.

During the Great Depression, food was very hard to come by so even a rotten banana was kept around. Paired with baking powder, a recipe was made up for banana bread. It was new to add mashed bananas into the recipe as a main ingredient instead of as a garnish.

Banana bread became so popular, there is even a day for it. National Banana Bread Day is on February 23rd.

According to My Great Recipes, The first banana bread recipe appeared in Pillsbury's Balanced Recipes Cookbook.

Cooking experts claim that banana bread is more like a cake than a bread because it does not contain any yeast.
