Eleanor & Park Review

Summary

Written by young adult American Author Rainbow Rowell, Eleanor and Park are two 16 year old misfits that stumble upon each other, creating a beautiful friendship and a first love experience.

Eleanor Douglas is new in school and one of the novel's main characters. She stands out in a crowd because of her curly red hair. She is very shy and does not let people in; she has a barrier because of past experiences of abuse and trauma.

Park Sheridan is another one of the novel's main characters. He is very artistic and doesn't really fit in with any of his peers. He is often seen listening to music or reading comic books.

Together, these "misfits" come together and find friendship and love all in one.

An Honest Review

I heard about this novel through a friend of mine; she loved it. As soon as I got my hands on it, I challenged myself to finish the entire book in one sitting. What I can tell you, is that it was the easiest and one of the best decisions I have ever made. I finished the novel in just about 12 hours. I smiled, I laughed, I even cried. It was so easy to read because it was just that good. I became invested in the lives of these teenagers because I was a teenager as well. I could relate to them; I felt for them. With every word I read, the more I became invested in their friendship and their first relationship. I would recommend this novel to any and every teenager that feels like they stick out from the regular crowd. With these misfits, you'll fit right in.

10/10
My Favorite Quotes

One of the most well known quotes from this novel is:

Then there's also just the other quotes that will make you swoon, cry, or maybe both.

"He tried to remember how this happened- how she went from someone he'd never met to the only one that mattered."

Critical Claims

Rainbow Rowell is an American author of young adult novels. Rowell published Eleanor & Park in 2013, which was named as the best young adult novel of the year by The New York Times. DreamWorks even reverted the rights to make the novel a movie. It has also been chosen as one of the 10 best books of 2013.

American author, John Green, also commented on this novel, leaving a lasting impression:

"Eleanor & Park reminded me not just what it's like to be young and in love with a girl, but also what it's like to be young and in love with a book."
